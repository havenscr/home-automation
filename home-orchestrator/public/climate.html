How the ladder works

The goal: as the apartment gets hotter relative to your target temp, the system progressively turns up cooling, in the order you choose. Lower rungs are quieter, higher rungs cool harder.

Heat pressure is the biggest gap between any room's current temp and the target. If office is 74F and kitchen is 73F with a 72F target, pressure = +2F (the office's gap).

Engagement: as pressure rises, more rungs engage. The thresholds are:

  • at target rung 1 only
  • +0.5F rungs 1-2
  • +1.5F rungs 1-3
  • +2.5F rungs 1-4
  • +3.5F all rungs

Per-AC fan speed: for each AC, the loop looks at every engaged rung that mentions it and uses the highest speed. So if rungs 1+2 say "kitchen low" and "kitchen medium", kitchen runs at MID. If a rung doesn't mention an AC, that AC isn't asked to ramp.

Hysteresis: when pressure drops, rungs disengage 0.5F below the threshold that engaged them. Prevents flapping at the boundary.

COOL vs FAN auto-switch: when a room drops below its target, the loop switches that AC from COOL to FAN (compressor off, just airflow). When the room rises back above target, it switches back to COOL. A 0.5F deadband on each side prevents the compressor from cycling every minute near setpoint. If you manually set an AC to DRY mode, the loop leaves it alone. It assumes that was intentional.

Two ladders, picked by clock: the office-quiet ladder is used Mon-Fri during your work hours (caps office fan to keep meetings quiet — kitchen does the heavy lifting). The default ladder runs all other times — evenings, weekends, overnight — when office can ramp to high (it's empty + cold air reaches the bedroom).

Example: Tuesday 11am, office hits 75F (+3F over target). Office-quiet ladder is active. Pressure 3F engages 4 of 4 rungs: kitchen low, office mid, kitchen mid, kitchen high. Office's max engaged speed is MID (rung 2). Kitchen's max is HIGH (rung 4). Result: office stays quiet at MID, kitchen ramps to HIGH to compensate. Exactly what you wanted.

Activity stats
Window
Counts pulled from the rolling activity log. Mode flips and fan changes above ~2/hour suggest the deadband or dwell is too tight; pressure peaks >3.5F repeatedly mean the system is genuinely fighting heat load (not a tuning issue).
HomeKit automation chain health
Tracks dummy switches that should fire daily (e.g. Routine Start, Fade Start Sunset). When a switch goes missing from the homebridge log for a day or more, the HomeKit-side automation that triggers it is likely broken (disabled, wrong condition, deleted). Catches broken chains within hours instead of waiting for the bi-weekly audit. Configure expectations in `config.homekitExpectations`.
Runaway-write breaker
If a slot does more than the threshold of writes inside the rolling window, the breaker engages a global pause for 30 minutes. Defense against a stuck loop burning the LG API quota.
Last manual override evidence
When the loop pauses an AC because it detected a setting change it didn't make, this shows what it saw. Helps tell a real human/app override apart from a spurious one (e.g. fan reset by the LG unit on a mode transition).
